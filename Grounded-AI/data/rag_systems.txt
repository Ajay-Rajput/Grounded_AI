Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines retrieval systems with large language models.

RAG works in three steps:
1. Chunk documents
2. Convert chunks into embeddings
3. Retrieve relevant chunks for a query

The retrieved context is passed to a language model to generate grounded answers.

Advantages of RAG:
- Reduces hallucination
- Improves factual accuracy
- Enables domain-specific knowledge integration

Top-K retrieval is commonly used to fetch the most relevant chunks.
